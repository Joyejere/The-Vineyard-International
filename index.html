Projects

White Petals Initiative

White Petals Initiative is a scheme established to empower and motivate Adolescent girls living in Slums. Female education is crucial for national development. The general belief is that “when you educate a man, you educate one but when you educate a woman, you educate a nation.” What White Petals seek to achieve is to empower girls with the Nigerian culture, technology and vocational skills. We seek to create a breeding ground for self discovery and emerging talents through motivation and mentorship.

We Cycle Slums

We raise awareness for better health practices in Makoko community such as personal hygiene, sanitation, waste management and recycling. We train slum residents on handling hazardous waste; provide safety equipment like boots, gloves, masks, coats, trousers and hats and set up health care schemes in collaboration with community hospitals. The aim is to improve the quality of life for poor people by providing access to clean water, improved sanitation, and waste management services; and supporting secure land tenure and affordable housing.

The Vine Sprouts

We aim to provide education, technical, vocational including business trainings for adolescents (ages 11-19), to help them create waste solutions, health and water sanitation in slums, startup their own businesses; piloting new technologies for waste processing, such as plastic smelters, pelletisers and paper-recycling machines.

About Us

People live in squatter settlements along the riverbanks and are exposed to health risks due to their poor living conditions.
Africa is home to 20 of the world's worst slums located in Kenya, Sierra Leone, Liberia, Ghana, Angola, Nigeria, South Africa and Egypt.
In many slums, a family of six will share a space no bigger than one small room. Our aim is to solve the sustainable development goals of eradicating poverty, creating sustainable cities & communities and achieving Gender equality. We hope to impact the urban slum of Makoko, Lagos Nigeria as a flag-ship project before spreading to other African slums. We want to improve the standard of living in Makoko sustainably; with the intention that future generations can have adequate access to basic infrastructure services such as electricity, water and sanitation, housing, or household waste collection.

SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS

CLEAN WATER & SANITATION

Makoko is home to people from variety of riverine communities along Nigeria’s coast. Access to clean water, electricity, schools and other vital amenities is scarce or nonexistent. In particular, the lack of clean water supply causes a multitude of problems – diseases, high infant mortality and a lack of time for education and work, because the procurement of water binds a lot of capacities. We Cycle Slum project will help improve the health status of residents through raising awareness of better health practices and provision of clean water and sanitation facilities.

SUSTAINABLE CITIES & COMMUNITIES

We Cycle Slums is an initiative of The Vine Yard International that seeeks to develop slums into Sustainable Cities. Our aim is to scale-up the delivery of basic infrastructure services for safe water, sanitation, better and affordable housing, waste removal and access to land tenure rights through collaborative efforts with local people and authorities

GENDER EQUALITY

Majority of children living in slums especially girls are deprived of their basic rights to education as a result of poverty, underdevelopment, mass illiteracy, ignorance, urban backwardness and poverty. White Petals Initiative has the sole goal of "Making Every Girl's Dream Come True!" With an education, girls will understand their rights, have a greater sense of what is needed to support health and wellbeing, and they will have greater opportunities to be employed in a fulfilling way and achieve their full potentials. White Petals seek to empower girls with the Nigerian culture, technology and vocational skills to create a breeding ground for self discovery and emerging talents through education, motivation and mentorship.

NO POVERTY

Most people living in slums live there not by their choice but as a result of poverty. The Vine Sprout is an initiative of The Vineyard International targeted at empowering adolescents with technology guided skills for community development. Training Centers will be available for youths to get vocational skills, develop innovative ideas and work together to create technology-aided solutions. Adolescents to be trained by The Vine Sprouts Initiative will be trained into young entrepreneurs and financed to start their own businesses. These includes sewing machines for sack production, incinerators for biodegradation of degradable wastes, tearing machines for plastic recycling, paper recycling machines and tools for wood work.
